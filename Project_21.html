Workflow Architecture
01

The Intake and Organization

Imagine a digital receptionist that never sleeps. In this first phase, the system instantly catches every incoming customer message, whether it arrives via a direct email or a website form (webhook). Because customer data can be messy, the workflow immediately cleans the formatting, normalizes the text, and even translates foreign languages into English. This ensures that by the time a ticket moves forward, it is perfectly formatted and ready for analysis, leaving zero administrative busywork for human agents.

/system/intake-layer/clean-and-normalize
02

The AI Brain

Once the ticket is clean and ready, it is handed over to our AI agent (powered by Anthropic or OpenAI). Instead of just reading keywords, the AI reads the room. It analyzes the context of the message to determine the customer's sentiment, the urgency of their problem, the specific support category, and even flags potential churn risks if a customer is highly frustrated. Think of this step as an empathetic triage nurse, instantly figuring out exactly who the customer is and how much help they need right now.

/system/ai-engine/classification-agent
03

Smart Routing and Resolution

This is where the analysis turns into a tangible customer experience. Based on the AI’s recommendation, the workflow acts as a smart traffic controller. If it is a standard inquiry, the system dynamically drafts a personalized AI reply and updates your CRM or helpdesk automatically. If the AI detects a high-risk or highly urgent ticket, it bypasses the auto-responder and immediately escalates it to the human support team via alerts. Meanwhile, a background observability layer quietly logs every metric, ensuring you always have a clear view of system performance and response quality.

/system/routing/resolution-and-escalation
